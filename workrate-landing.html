The problem

Freelancing runs on trust.
Trust is hard to prove.

Every freelancer has been there — a client who questions the hours, a payment that stalls, a relationship that frays. It's not about dishonesty. It's about the absence of evidence.

"Can you prove you worked 8 hours?"
Clients can't see inside your screen. A time-tracking number with no evidence behind it is just a number — and some clients know it.
Payment disputes kill momentum
Chasing invoices, re-explaining work, offering discounts to keep the peace. Every billing dispute costs you time, money, and the relationship.
Screenshots feel like surveillance
Existing "proof" tools take screenshots of your screen. Invasive, uncomfortable, and exposing confidential client work to a third party.
How it works

Register tabs. Start timer.
Work normally.

WorkRate only counts seconds when you're actively on a tab you registered for that project. The moment you leave — clock pauses. The moment you return — clock resumes.

Register your project tabs

Before you start, register the tabs you'll actually work in — Figma, GitHub, your dev server, Notion. The extension only counts time on those tabs.

Work normally — it auto-tracks

Switch to Gmail? Clock pauses — logged as "off-tab." Walk away from your desk? Clock pauses — logged as "idle." Come back to Figma? Clock resumes instantly.

Send your client a proof report

At session end, share a link. Your client sees verified time, the breakdown, which tabs you were on, and a WQI score — all without seeing anything sensitive.

Registered-tab tracking
